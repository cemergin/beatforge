# Electronic Music History — Step-Sequencer Patterns
## Patterns for Genres from the Electronic Music History Report
## Playable Patterns for BeatForge Web Drum Machine

### Notation Key
- Each character = one subdivision position in the cycle
- 16-step patterns = 4/4 time (grouped 4+4+4+4)
- 32-step patterns = two bars of 4/4 (grouped 4+4+4+4|4+4+4+4)
- `x` = hit (normal velocity), `X` = accented hit, `.` = rest
- `G` = ghost note (very low velocity), `o` = open hi-hat, `+` = closed/muted
- Pipe `|` marks beat divisions
- KK = kick, SN = snare, HH = closed hi-hat, OH = open hi-hat
- CL = clap, TM = tom, CB = cowbell, SH = shaker, RS = rimshot
- CG = conga, PR = perc (generic), CR = crash, RD = ride
- 808 = 808 sub-bass, SY = synth stab, AC = acid (TB-303)
- CP = clap, RM = rim

### Velocity Layers
- `X` (accent) = velocity 110-127
- `x` (normal hit) = velocity 80-100
- `G` (ghost note) = velocity 25-45
- `o` (open hi-hat) = velocity 75-95

### === 1. SYNTH-POP CLASSIC (Depeche Mode / New Order Style) ===
Genre: Synth-Pop
Origin: UK (Sheffield, Basildon, Manchester), 1979-1986
BPM: 118-126
Time Sig: 4/4
Steps: 16
Difficulty: Beginner
Description: The foundational synth-pop beat. LinnDrum or TR-808 providing the backbone, with crisp electronic clap on 2 and 4, and driving 16th-note hi-hats. The pattern is deliberately mechanical — machine-precision was the aesthetic, not a limitation. Think Depeche Mode "Just Can't Get Enough," New Order "Blue Monday" (without the syncopation), Human League "Don't You Want Me."

**Basic Pattern (16 steps):**

```
HH:  |x.x.|x.x.|x.x.|x.x.|  Straight 8th-note hi-hats
CP:  |....|X...|....|X...|  Electronic clap on 2 and 4
KK:  |X...|..X.|X...|..X.|  Kick on 1 and the "and" of 2
SY:  |X...|....|X...|....|  Synth stab on downbeats
```

**Extended with 16th Hats (32 steps):**

```
HH:  |x.x.x.x.|x.x.x.x.|x.x.x.x.|x.x.x.x.|
```

### === 2. NEW ORDER "BLUE MONDAY" STYLE ===
Genre: Synth-Pop / Proto-Dance
Origin: Manchester, UK, 1983
BPM: 130
Time Sig: 4/4
Steps: 32 (two bars — the pattern needs both bars to resolve)
Difficulty: Intermediate
Description: The best-selling 12-inch single of all time. Stephen Morris programmed this on an Oberheim DMX. The kick pattern is the key: a syncopated pattern that pulls against the steady hi-hats, creating tension and drive. The pattern bridges synth-pop and dance music — it could be played alongside house records, which is exactly what happened in 1983-1988.

### === 3. GARY NUMAN / TUBEWAY ARMY STYLE ===
Genre: Synth-Pop / Electronic
Origin: London, UK, 1979
BPM: 114-120
Time Sig: 4/4

Difficulty: Beginner

### === 4. EBM BASIC (DAF / Front 242 Style) ===
Genre: Electronic Body Music
Origin: Belgium / Germany, 1981-1988
BPM: 120-135
Time Sig: 4/4
Steps: 16
Difficulty: Beginner
Description: The quintessential EBM beat. Pounding four-on-the-floor kick, aggressive electronic snare or clap on 2 and 4, driving sequenced bass. The rhythmic template is similar to house music, but the timbre is harder, more distorted, and the overall energy is aggressive rather than euphoric. DAF's "Der Mussolini," Front 242's "Headhunter," Nitzer Ebb's "Join in the Chant."

```
HH:  |x.x.x.x.|x.x.x.x.|  Relentless 16th hats
CP:  |....|X...|....|X...|  Hard clap on 2 and 4
KK:  |X...|X...|X...|X...|  Four-on-the-floor (distorted kick)
SY:  |x.x.|.x.x|x.x.|.x.x|  Sequenced synth bass (shifting accent)
```

Origin: Cleveland/Chicago, USA, 1989-1994

Time Sig: 4/4

### === 7. ITALO DISCO (Alexander Robotnick / Klein & MBO Style) ===
Genre: Italo Disco
Origin: Italy, 1980-1988
BPM: 118-128
Time Sig: 4/4
Steps: 16
Difficulty: Beginner
Description: The overlooked ancestor of house and techno. TR-808 drums, synth bass arpeggio, and a characteristic "bounce" created by the interplay of kick and bass. KK:  |X...|X...|X...|X...|  Four-on-the-floor


### === 8. HI-NRG (Bobby O / Patrick Cowley Style) ===
Genre: Hi-NRG
Origin: San Francisco / New York, USA, 1978-1986
BPM: 128-140
Time Sig: 4/4
Steps: 16
Difficulty: Beginner
Description: The gay club sound that bridged disco and house. Faster than disco, more electronic, pounding four-on-the-floor with heavy emphasis on the kick. Patrick Cowley's productions for Sylvester ("You Make Me Feel Mighty Real") and Bobby Orlando's work for Divine created a relentless, euphoric sound. The hi-hat pattern is often open on every off-beat, creating a "breathing" effect.

```
HH:  |x...|x...|x...|x...|  Quarter-note closed hats
OH:  |..o.|..o.|..o.|..o.|  Open hat on every "and"
CP:  |....|X...|....|X...|  Clap on 2 and 4
KK:  |X...|X...|X...|X...|  Pounding four-on-the-floor
SH:  |x.x.x.x.|x.x.x.x.|  Shaker 16ths for energy
```

Genre: Deep House
Origin: Chicago, USA, 1985-1990
BPM: 118-122
Time Sig: 4/4
Steps: 16
Difficulty: Beginner
Description: The warm, soulful side of house music. Larry Heard's "Can You Feel It" and "Mystery of Love" defined the template: a gentle, swinging kick pattern with soft percussion, jazzy chord voicings from a Juno-106, and an overall mood of introspective warmth. The hi-hat pattern has a slight swing that distinguishes deep house from the straighter grid of other house styles.

```
SH:  |x.x.x.x.|x.x.x.x.|  Light shaker
CG:  |..x.|....|..x.|.x..|  Conga accents (subtle)
```

# MINIMAL TECHNO / MICROHOUSE

---

### === 12. MINIMAL TECHNO (Richie Hawtin / Plastikman Style) ===
Genre: Minimal Techno
Origin: Detroit / Berlin, 1993-2010
BPM: 125-132
Time Sig: 4/4
Steps: 16
Difficulty: Intermediate
Description: Reduction as aesthetic statement. Richie Hawtin's Plastikman project stripped techno to its barest elements — a kick, a hi-hat, maybe one more element. The groove comes from micro-timing variations and the subtle interplay between just 2-3 elements. Robert Hood's "Minimal Nation" is the blueprint. The pattern below is deliberately sparse — that's the point.

```
HH:  |..x.|..x.|..x.|..x.|  Sparse hi-hat on "and" of 1 and 3
CP:  |....|....|....|..x.|  Clap only at end of bar (or removed)
KK:  |X...|X...|X...|X...|  Steady four-on-the-floor
PR:  |....|.x..|....|....|  Single percussion element (click, pop)
```

**With Polyrhythmic Overlay (32 steps):**

```
HH:  |..x.|..x.|..x.|..x.|..x.|..x.|..x.|..x.|
CP:  |....|....|....|..x.|....|....|....|....|
KK:  |X...|X...|X...|X...|X...|X...|X...|X...|
PR:  |....|.x..|....|....|.x..|....|....|.x..|
RS:  |.....x....|.....x....|  (5-step loop against 16-step grid)
```

**Note:** The rimshot (RS) has a 5-step cycle against the 16-step grid, creating a shifting polyrhythmic relationship. This technique — layering loops of different lengths — is fundamental to minimal techno.

# BERLIN SCHOOL / PROGRESSIVE ELECTRONIC

---

### === 20. MOTORIK / NEU! BEAT ===
Genre: Krautrock / Motorik
Origin: Düsseldorf, Germany, 1971-1975
BPM: 110-130
Time Sig: 4/4
Steps: 16
Difficulty: Beginner
Description: Not strictly "electronic music" but the rhythmic foundation for much of it. "Motorik" (German: "motor skill") describes the driving, hypnotic beat invented by **Klaus Dinger** of Neu! and later adopted by Kraftwerk. The pattern is deceptively simple: a steady kick on every beat with alternating snare (or hi-hat) hits creating a propulsive, trance-like feel. Its simplicity IS its genius — it creates a sense of forward motion that can sustain for 20+ minutes.

```
HH:  |x.x.|x.x.|x.x.|x.x.|  Straight 8th-note pattern
SN:  |....|X...|....|X...|  Snare on 2 and 4
KK:  |X...|X...|X...|X...|  Every quarter note
```

**With Hi-Hat Variation (the "Apache" motorik):**

```
HH:  |x..x|x..x|x..x|x..x|  Dotted-eighth pattern
OH:  |..o.|....|..o.|....|  Open hat accent
SN:  |....|X...|....|X...|  Snare on 2 and 4
KK:  |X...|X...|X...|X...|  Every quarter note
```
