# Celtic, Northern European, Romani & Klezmer Pattern Library
## Playable Step-Sequencer Patterns for Web Drum Machine

### How to Read This Document

Each position in the grid is one **16th note**. A measure of 4/4 = 16 positions.
Compound meters: 6/8 = 12 positions (four groups of 3 eighth notes shown as 6 sixteenth notes per group).
Odd meters are handled by adjusting the total grid length (e.g., 9/8 = 18 sixteenth notes, 3/8 = 6 sixteenth notes).

**Bodhran Voices (Irish/Scottish):**
- `D` = Bass tone (center of skin, open hand behind) -- low, resonant
- `T` = Treble (edge strike / tip of tipper) -- bright, high
- `R` = Rim shot (tipper strikes rim and skin simultaneously)
- `G` = Ghost note (very light stroke, barely audible)

**General Percussion Mapping:**
- `KK` = Kick drum (maps from bass tones)
- `SN` = Snare / rimshot (maps from treble / accented strokes)
- `HH` = Hi-hat (maps from ghost notes / continuous pulse)

**Symbols:**
- `x` = strike (normal velocity)
- `X` = accented strike (high velocity)
- `.` = rest / silence
- `|` = beat or group division (visual only)
- Pipe groups show metric subdivisions

## 6. Scottish Strathspey
```
Genre: Scottish Traditional / Highland Dance
Origin: Scotland (Strath Spey, Highland)
BPM: 130-152 (moderate — must be slow enough for Scotch snap to register)
Time Sig: 4/4 (16 sixteenth notes)
Difficulty: Intermediate
Tags: Scottish, Highland, Scotch snap, dotted, dance
Description: The strathspey is unique to Scotland, characterized by the "Scotch snap"
— a short accented note followed by a longer unaccented one (sixteenth + dotted
eighth). This REVERSES the normal dotted rhythm. The pattern alternates between
normal dotted figures (long-short) and Scotch snaps (short-LONG), creating a
distinctive push-pull tension. The snare pattern reflects pipe band drumming
with flams and drags on accent points.

Note: "s" positions represent the Scotch snap (SHORT-long), "d" positions
represent normal dotted rhythm (LONG-short). The grid below shows the snare
drum interpretation.

Snare (pipe band style) — 16 positions [4|4|4|4]:
SN: |X..x|X..x|x.X.|X..x|
    (snap) (dot) (snap)(dot)

Kit Adaptation (KK/SN/HH):
KK: |X...|....|X...|....|
SN: |x..x|X..x|x.X.|X..x|
HH: |x..x|x..x|x..x|x..x|

Note: The Scotch snap feel requires specific quantization. Positions 1-2 in
each snap group should be weighted approximately 1:3 (short:long). Apply this
at the playback engine level for authentic feel.

## 8. Swedish Polska
```
Genre: Swedish Folk / Couple Dance
Origin: Sweden (Dalarna, Uppland, Västmanland regions)
BPM: 100-140 (varies by region; dotted quarter = beat)
Time Sig: 3/4 ASYMMETRIC (12 sixteenth notes, but beats are unequal)
Difficulty: Advanced
Tags: Scandinavian, Swedish, polska, asymmetric, triple meter, dance
Description: The Swedish polska is in 3/4 but with UNEQUAL beat lengths. In the
slängpolska tradition, beat 2 is delayed (lengthened), creating a short-LONG-
medium feel approximately 2:4:3 in eighth-note equivalents. The asymmetry is
not imprecision — it is deliberate and consistent, tied to the dance movement.
Typically played on nyckelharpa (keyed fiddle) or fiddle with no dedicated
percussion. This pattern extracts the rhythmic skeleton for drum machine use.

Note: Notated in 12 positions (standard 3/4), but playback should apply
asymmetric timing: beat 1 shortened (~83%), beat 2 lengthened (~133%),
beat 3 normal (~100%). BeatForge implementation note: requires custom
beat-length array [0.83, 1.33, 1.0] relative to equal subdivision.

Rhythmic Skeleton — 12 positions [4|4|4]:
KK: |X...|....|....|
SN: |....|X...|..x.|
HH: |x.x.|x.x.|x.x.|

Variant (heavier, with foot stomp on 1):
KK: |X...|....|x...|
SN: |....|X..x|....|
HH: |x..x|x..x|x..x|

CRITICAL: Without asymmetric beat timing, this will sound like a standard
waltz. The polska's identity IS the asymmetry.
```

## 13. Gypsy Jazz "La Pompe"
```
Genre: Jazz Manouche / Gypsy Jazz
Origin: France / Belgium (1930s, Django Reinhardt)
BPM: 120-240 (wide range; medium swing ~160)
Time Sig: 4/4 (16 sixteenth notes)
Difficulty: Intermediate
Tags: Romani, Manouche, Sinti, gypsy jazz, la pompe, swing, Django
Description: "La pompe" (the pump) is the percussive rhythm guitar technique that
REPLACES drums in gypsy jazz. Heavily accenting beats 2 and 4 (like swing),
with ghost strokes on 1 and 3. The quick upstroke after beats 2 and 4 gives
the signature "chunk-A, chunk-A" sound. This pattern translates the guitar's
rhythmic role into a percussion grid. Note: In authentic gypsy jazz there
are NO drums — this is an adaptation for creative/educational purposes.

La Pompe as Percussion — 16 positions [4|4|4|4]:
Ghost:   |G...|....|G...|....|  (beats 1,3 — muted/light)
Accent:  |....|X..x|....|X..x|  (beats 2,4 — heavy + upstroke)

## Asymmetric Beat Timing (Polska/Springar)

The Scandinavian polska and springar traditions require **non-equal beat subdivision**
within 3/4 time. Standard step sequencers assume equal beat lengths. To authentically
represent these traditions, BeatForge needs:

1. A "beat length array" parameter per pattern: e.g., `[0.83, 1.33, 1.0]` for Swedish
   polska, where each value is a multiplier on the standard beat duration.
2. The visual grid should still show 3 equal-width columns (for intuitive editing),
   but playback stretches/compresses each beat according to the array.
3. Possible UI: a small "asymmetry" slider or preset for each beat within the measure.

## Dotted/Swing Quantization

Irish hornpipes, Scottish strathspeys, Hungarian dotted rhythms, and gypsy jazz all
require some form of swing or dotted-note quantization:

- **Hornpipe/Hungarian:** ~66% swing (dotted eighth + sixteenth ratio)
- **Strathspey Scotch snap:** Reversed — sixteenth on the beat, dotted eighth after
- **Gypsy jazz la pompe:** ~58-62% swing (lighter than hornpipe)

BeatForge should offer a "swing" parameter (50% = straight, 66% = dotted, with
continuous adjustment) and ideally a "reverse swing" option for Scotch snaps.

## Patterns Without Traditional Percussion

Many Celtic/European traditions have NO dedicated percussion instrument. The patterns
above extract rhythmic skeletons from:
- Melodic accent patterns (Scandinavian polska)
- Dance step rhythms (csárdás, verbunkos)
- Guitar accompaniment patterns (la pompe)
- Implied metric frameworks (klezmer)

In the BeatForge Library, these patterns should be clearly labeled as "rhythmic
adaptations" rather than transcriptions of existing drum parts.
