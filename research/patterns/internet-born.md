# Internet-Born Genre Drum Patterns
## For Web Drum Machine Step Sequencer

**Notation Key:**
- `x` = hit
- `X` = accent (hard hit)
- `.` = rest (silence)
- `o` = open hi-hat
- `g` = ghost note (soft hit)
- `+` = closed/muted
- `|` = barline
- Each character = one 16th note (unless noted otherwise)
- Steps 1, 5, 9, 13 = beats 1, 2, 3, 4 in 4/4 time

**Instruments:**
- `KK` = kick/bass drum
- `SN` = snare drum
- `CP` = clap
- `CH` = closed hi-hat
- `OH` = open hi-hat
- `RS` = rimshot / cross-stick
- `CB` = cowbell
- `PR` = perc (general)
- `SB` = sub bass / 808
- `CR` = crash cymbal
- `RD` = ride cymbal
- `SH` = shaker
- `TM` = tambourine
- `CW` = cowbell (808)
- `NZ` = noise / texture
- `VX` = vocal chop

**Velocity Layers:**
- `X` (accent) = velocity 110-127
- `x` (normal hit) = velocity 80-100
- `g` (ghost note) = velocity 25-45
- `o` (open hi-hat) = velocity 75-95

# VAPORWAVE FAMILY

---

### === 1. Vaporwave / Mallsoft ===
Genre: Vaporwave
Origin: Bandcamp/Tumblr, 2010-2011
BPM: 70 (pitched down from ~100 BPM source)
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: The iconic slowed-and-pitched-down drum pattern of vaporwave. Imagine this playing through the PA system of an empty shopping mall at 3 AM. All sounds should be drenched in reverb with dulled transients — low-pass filter everything. Drums from LinnDrum or Oberheim DMX, pitched down. Ghost notes barely audible.

```
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |................|
SN: |....x.......x...|
KK: |x.......x.x.....|
```

Notes: Apply heavy reverb (3-5 second tail), low-pass filter at 3-5kHz on all drums. Reduce hi-hat velocity to ~40%. The entire pattern should sound submerged, distant, and slightly unstable in timing. Add vinyl crackle layer.

---

### === 2. Future Funk ===
Genre: Future Funk
Origin: Bandcamp/YouTube, 2012-2013
BPM: 122
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: The dancefloor-ready vaporwave descendant. Four-on-the-floor kick with aggressive sidechain compression creating the signature "pumping" effect. Inspired by French house (Daft Punk) meets Japanese city pop sampling. Pair with anime visuals from 1980s Macross or City Hunter.

```
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |..........o.....|
CP: |....X.......X...|
KK: |X...X...X...X...|
SH: |..x...x...x...x.|
```

Notes: Heavy sidechain compression triggered by kick — all elements duck when kick hits, creating the pumping feel. Open hi-hat on the "and" of beat 3 for disco flavor. Add filter sweep automation (low-pass from 500Hz to 15kHz over 8 bars). Clap should be bright and snappy, contrasting the sidechain-compressed melodic elements.

# LO-FI BEATS

### === 3. Lo-fi Hip-Hop Standard (The Lofi Girl Beat) ===
Genre: Lo-fi Hip-Hop
Origin: YouTube (Lofi Girl stream), ~2017
BPM: 78
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Intermediate
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: THE beat that 50,000+ concurrent YouTube viewers studied to. J Dilla-influenced "drunken" timing — notes intentionally fall slightly before or after the grid. Nujabes-inspired jazz sampling aesthetic. This pattern should feel like a warm blanket — comforting, repetitive, slightly imperfect. Apply vinyl crackle and tape hiss as constant texture layers.

```
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |......o.........|
SN: |....x..g....X..g|
KK: |X..g......x.X...|
RS: |................|
SH: |.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
```

Notes: CRITICAL — apply 65% swing to hi-hats and shaker. Nudge snare on beat 2 slightly late (+10ms). Nudge kick on "and" of beat 3 slightly early (-8ms). Ghost notes on snare (g) at ~30% velocity. Apply low-pass filter at 8kHz on entire drum bus. Add vinyl crackle layer at constant low volume. Kick should be warm and rounded (remove attack transient). Snare should sound like it was recorded through an old cassette player.

### === 4. Lo-fi House ===
Genre: Lo-fi House
Origin: Bandcamp/SoundCloud, 2015-2016
BPM: 115
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: DJ Seinfeld, Ross From Friends, Mall Grab — classic house patterns recorded to cassette tape and played back through degraded equipment. The wow and flutter of tape creates subtle timing instability. This should sound like a 909 that's been left in a damp basement for 20 years.

```
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |..o...o...o...o.|
CP: |....x.......x...|
KK: |X...X...X...X...|
```

Notes: Route all drums through tape saturation — bit-crush at 12-bit, add wow/flutter modulation to timing (subtle pitch wobble). Open hi-hat on every offbeat (classic house pattern). Clap should be soft, rounded, with long reverb tail. Kick is four-on-the-floor but with reduced attack — warm and thuddy rather than punchy. Apply gentle low-pass filter at 10kHz. The overall sound should be "warm" and "worn."

### === 5. Chillhop ===
Genre: Chillhop
Origin: Spotify/YouTube playlists, ~2014
BPM: 87
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Intermediate
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: When a playlist brand becomes a genre. Chillhop Music (Netherlands) curated a sound that thousands of producers adopted. Jazzier and slightly more polished than lo-fi hip-hop, with live-sounding drums (brushes, jazz ride). The soundtrack of coffee shops and co-working spaces worldwide.

```
RD: |x..x.xx..x.xx..x|
CH: |..x...x...x...x.|
SN: |..g.X.g.g...X.g.|
KK: |X.....x...X.....|
SH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
```

Notes: Use ride cymbal (jazz-style, light touch) as primary timekeeper with triplet-feel hits. Snare uses cross-stick/rimshot sound. Ghost notes on snare at ~35% velocity create jazz-influenced texture. Apply moderate swing (55-60%). Bass drum is round and warm. Shaker provides constant gentle texture. Cleaner than lo-fi hip-hop — warm but not degraded. Walking bass line recommended as accompaniment.

### === 6. Nightcore ===
Genre: Nightcore
Origin: YouTube/anime communities, 2002→
BPM: 175 (sped up from ~140 BPM eurodance source)
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: Originally a Norwegian school project that became a global phenomenon. Any trance or eurodance track sped up 25% with pitch raised accordingly. Paired with anime character images on YouTube. The high BPM aligns with happy hardcore territory. Vocals become unnaturally bright and childlike from the pitch shift.

```
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |..o...o...o...o.|
CP: |....X.......X...|
KK: |X...X...X...X...|
CR: |X...............|
```

Notes: Four-on-the-floor at 175 BPM — this is FAST. All drum sounds should be pitch-shifted up (+3 semitones from normal). Kicks become tight and clicky (short decay). Snares/claps become thin and bright. Hi-hats shimmer at high frequencies. Add crash on beat 1 of every 4 or 8 bars. The pattern is simple — the speed IS the complexity. This is essentially eurodance/trance at 1.25x speed.

### === 7. Chopped & Screwed / Slowed + Reverb ===
Genre: Slowed + Reverb (Internet revival)
Origin: YouTube, 2017 (building on DJ Screw's 1990s Houston legacy)
BPM: 62 (slowed from ~90 BPM original)
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: DJ Screw (Houston, TX) invented the original technique in the early 1990s, releasing 300+ Screw Tapes. The internet revival (Slater, 2017) simplified it to "slow down + add reverb." Controversial on TikTok when creators failed to credit DJ Screw's legacy. The slowed tempo creates vast, contemplative spaces between beats. Every hit becomes an event.

```
CH: |x...x...x...x...|
OH: |................|
SN: |........X.......|
KK: |X...........x...|
SB: |X...........x...|
```

Notes: EXTREMELY slow and sparse. Pitch everything DOWN (-3 to -5 semitones). Apply massive reverb (5-8 second tail) to all elements. Kicks become sub-bass thuds. Snares become deep, cavernous booms. Hi-hats play quarter notes only (eighth notes at this tempo would be too fast for the aesthetic). The space between hits is vast and contemplative. 808 sub-bass slides slowly between notes. The original "chopped" technique also involved scratching and skipping — program occasional stutter/repeat effects on the snare.

### === 8. PC Music / SOPHIE-Style ===
Genre: PC Music
Origin: SoundCloud/London, 2013
BPM: 140
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Intermediate
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: A.G. Cook's PC Music label and SOPHIE's radical sound design created hyper-synthetic, post-ironic pop. SOPHIE described her sounds in terms of materials — latex, metal, plastic, liquid. Percussion should sound like manufactured objects from a cartoon world. Everything is hyper-compressed with zero dynamic range. This is pop music pushed to absurd, beautiful extremes.

```
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |......o.......o.|
CP: |....X.......X...|
KK: |X..x..x.X..x..x.|
PR: |..x.......x.....|
```

Notes: EXTREME compression on drum bus — every hit at maximum volume, zero dynamic range. Use synthetic percussion sounds — not emulating any real drum machine. Think: breaking glass, popping bubbles, metallic ping, plastic snap. Kick should be tight and synthetic. Clap should be razor-sharp and hyper-bright. Hi-hats should be crystalline. Add occasional 64th-note stutter fills on the clap (rapid-fire glitch rolls). The pattern is structurally conventional (pop placement) — the radical quality is entirely in the sound design and processing.

### === 9. Hyperpop / 100 gecs Style ===
Genre: Hyperpop
Origin: Spotify playlist / SoundCloud, 2019
BPM: 155
Time Sig: 4/4 (loosely)
Difficulty: Advanced
Steps: 32 (two bars — the pattern shifts between sections)
Cultural Context: 100 gecs' 1000 gecs (2019) was 23 minutes of distorted ska-punk, nightcore, dubstep, and happy hardcore blended in a sonic blender set on fire. Spotify coined the term "hyperpop" by creating a playlist. The genre treats ALL genres as raw material. This pattern represents the chaotic, genre-switching nature — it changes feel mid-pattern.

```
BAR 1 (trap-influenced):
CH: |x.xxX.x.x.xxX.x.|
OH: |......o.........|
CP: |....X.......X...|
KK: |X.........X.x...|
SB: |X.........X.x...|

BAR 2 (punk-influenced shift):
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |................|
SN: |....X...X...X..X|
KK: |X...X.X.X...X.X.|
CR: |X...............|
```

Notes: Bar 1 is trap-influenced — rolling hi-hats with velocity accents, distorted 808 bass. Bar 2 shifts to a punk/hardcore feel — faster kick pattern, more snare hits, crash cymbal. THIS GENRE-SHIFT WITHIN A PATTERN IS THE POINT. Apply extreme distortion and saturation to all elements. 808 bass should clip and buzz. Hi-hat rolls should be bitcrushed. The overall mix should be hot/clipping — hyperpop embraces digital distortion as aesthetic. Layer multiple drum sounds simultaneously for maximum density.

### === 10. Glitchcore ===
Genre: Glitchcore
Origin: SoundCloud/TikTok, ~2019
BPM: 170 (unstable — varies within pattern)
Time Sig: Irregular
Difficulty: Expert
Steps: 32 (two bars of chaos)
Cultural Context: The extreme endpoint of hyperpop. Breakcore meets TikTok audio manipulation. Amen break chopped beyond recognition, bit-crushed into oblivion. Tracks by Alice Gas, p4rkr, 8485. The drum pattern should sound like a broken audio file trying to play multiple genres simultaneously.

```
BAR 1:
CH: |x.xX.xx..Xx.x.x.|
OH: |.....o..........|
SN: |..X.x..X.x..X.X.|
KK: |X..x.X....X..x..|
NZ: |...x........x...|

BAR 2:
CH: |..x..x..........|
OH: |........o.......|
SN: |X.xX..X.X.xX..X.|
KK: |..X..x..X.X....x|
NZ: |x...x...x...x...|
```

Notes: This pattern is DELIBERATELY broken. Insert random silences (drop all instruments for 1-2 16th notes). Apply extreme bit-crushing (8-bit or lower). Use amen break samples chopped to individual hits. Timing should be INTENTIONALLY inconsistent — some hits quantized, others wildly off-grid. NZ (noise) bursts function as percussion. Add stutter/glitch effects that repeat single hits at 64th or 128th note speeds. The overall feel should be "malfunctioning software" — not random, but deliberately unstable.

### === 12. Classic Phonk (Memphis Style) ===
Genre: Phonk (Classic)
Origin: SoundCloud, ~2012 (reviving 1990s Memphis rap)
BPM: 70 (half-time, effective hi-hat tempo: 140 BPM)
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Intermediate
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: Internet producers reviving the sound of early 1990s Memphis rap cassettes (Three 6 Mafia, DJ Spanish Fly, Tommy Wright III). The original tapes were nearly impossible to find until fans digitized and uploaded them to YouTube. SoundCloud phonk producers (DJ Smokey, Soudiere) sampled these uploads, processing them through additional lo-fi degradation. Without the internet archiving obscure Memphis cassettes, this genre could not exist.

```
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |..............o.|
SN: |........X.......|
KK: |X..x......x.....|
CW: |..x...x.x.x...x.|
VX: |............x...|
```

Notes: HALF-TIME feel — snare hits on beat 3 only (not 2 and 4). Hi-hats play at double the tempo feel (140 BPM worth of 16ths over the 70 BPM pulse). The 808 cowbell (CW) plays a MELODIC pattern — it functions as the primary melodic element, bit-crushed and saturated. VX = vocal chop from Memphis rap sample, used as rhythmic punctuation. Apply heavy lo-fi processing: low-pass filter at 6kHz, tape saturation, bit-crushing. Add significant swing to hi-hats (60-65% shuffle). The overall sound should be murky, dark, and deliberately degraded.

### === 13. Drift Phonk (KORDHELL Style) ===
Genre: Drift Phonk
Origin: YouTube/TikTok, ~2020
BPM: 138
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: KORDHELL's "Murder In My Mind" and "Live Another Day" soundtracked millions of car drift compilation videos on YouTube and TikTok. The music and the drift video format co-evolved — each shaped the other. Massive popularity in Russia, Middle East, and Southeast Asia alongside American origins. The cowbell is EVERYTHING — louder than any other element, distorted and saturated to the extreme.

```
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |..............o.|
CP: |....X.......X...|
KK: |X...X...X...X...|
CW: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
SB: |X.......X.......|
```

Notes: The cowbell (CW) plays RELENTLESS eighth notes — it IS the genre. Distort the cowbell heavily (saturation, bit-crushing, overdrive). It should be the LOUDEST element in the mix. Four-on-the-floor kick provides driving momentum. Clap on 2 and 4 with maximum impact. 808 sub-bass hits hard on beat 1 of each bar with pitch slides. Apply extreme saturation and clipping to the entire mix — the mix should be deliberately "hot." Everything should feel like it's being blasted through a car's subwoofer system at dangerous volumes.

### === 14. Brazilian Phonk ===
Genre: Brazilian Phonk / Funk
Origin: TikTok/YouTube, ~2021
BPM: 145
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Intermediate
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: The collision of American drift phonk with Brazilian baile funk (favela funk from Rio de Janeiro) through the internet. The tamborzao beat — the rhythmic heartbeat of the favelas — meets 808 cowbell and distorted bass. Exploded through gym/fitness TikTok content. MC GW bridges both worlds. Agogo bells and woodblocks from Brazilian percussion tradition add rhythmic complexity. The bass is designed to be felt physically.

```
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |................|
CP: |..x...x...x...x.|
KK: |X..x..X.X..x..X.|
CW: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
SB: |X..x..X.X..x..X.|
PR: |.x..x..x.x..x..x|
```

Notes: The clap placement is KEY — it sits on OFFBEATS, not the standard 2 and 4 position. This syncopated clap pattern comes from the tamborzao tradition and gives Brazilian phonk its distinctive bounce. Kick pattern is more syncopated than standard phonk — follows the baile funk groove. Cowbell plays eighth notes (shared with drift phonk). PR = agogo bell or woodblock adding extra syncopation. 808 bass follows the kick pattern with extreme sub-bass weight and pitch slides. Apply distortion and saturation to everything. The overall feel should be bouncy, aggressive, and physically impactful.

Genre: Gym Phonk

Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: A functional subset of phonk optimized specifically for workout content. Takes the most aggressive elements of drift phonk and pushes them further — maximum rhythmic intensity, zero subtlety, no breakdowns or ambient sections. Designed to accompany heavy lifting, HIIT training, and PR attempts. The cowbell is even more relentless than drift phonk.

```
CH: |xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx|
OH: |................|

KK: |X.x.X.x.X.x.X.x.|

Kick drum plays an aggressive, active pattern — more hits than standard. No ghost notes, no subtlety, no dynamics. Everything at maximum velocity. Apply extreme distortion, saturation, and limiting. The mix should be a solid wall of rhythmic force. No breakdowns, no builds — just relentless energy from start to finish.

### === 16. SoundCloud Rap ===
Genre: SoundCloud Rap
Origin: SoundCloud, 2015-2018
BPM: 140 (or 70 BPM half-time)
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: Lil Pump, XXXTentacion, Smokepurpp, Lil Peep — teenagers who built millions of fans entirely through SoundCloud without label support or radio play. The production is deliberately raw: distorted 808s, minimal arrangement, lo-fi quality. Short songs (often under 2 minutes). The distorted 808 bass IS the genre — pushed to extreme levels of saturation and clipping, it dominates the mix and functions as both bass and lead instrument.

```
CH: |x.xXx.x.x.xXx.x.|
OH: |..............o.|
CP: |....X.......X...|
KK: |X.........X.....|
SB: |X...x.x...X.x...|
```

Notes: The 808 (SB) is the STAR — apply extreme distortion, saturation, and boost. It should buzz and clip. Program 808 with pitch slides between notes (glide). Hi-hats have trap-style rolls with velocity accents. Clap on 2 and 4, simple. Kick triggers at the start of each 808 note. Arrangement is SPARSE — kick, clap, hat, 808, and that's it. Large amounts of empty space for aggressive vocal delivery. The lo-fi quality is intentional — clipping, poor mix balance, and limited frequency range are part of the aesthetic. Keep songs short.

### === 17. Bedroom Pop ===
Genre: Bedroom Pop
Origin: YouTube/Spotify, ~2015-2017
BPM: 98
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: Clairo's "Pretty Girl" (2017, recorded on webcam, 40M+ views) epitomizes the aesthetic — music literally made in bedrooms with consumer-grade equipment. boy pablo, Gus Dapperton, Rex Orange County. The production imperfections ARE the aesthetic: room noise, cheap mic quality, timing that rushes and drags. The "bedroom" signifies authenticity and rejection of professional slickness.

```
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |................|
SN: |....x.......x...|
KK: |x.......x.......|
TM: |..x...x...x...x.|
```

Notes: Use a drum machine sound (Roland TR-505 or Boss DR-series emulation) but program with LOOSE timing — nudge hits slightly off-grid (+/- 5-15ms randomly). Snare should be muffled and gentle (low-pass filter, reduce attack). Kick is warm but not powerful. Tambourine adds gentle texture. Overall velocity is LOW — nothing hits hard. Apply room reverb (small room, short tail) to suggest an actual bedroom recording environment. Embrace imperfection: slightly uneven velocity, timing drift. The pattern should sound like someone programming a cheap drum machine for the first time and getting it charmingly almost-right.

### === 18. Cloud Rap ===
Genre: Cloud Rap
Origin: SoundCloud/Tumblr, ~2010
BPM: 73
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Intermediate
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: Lil B coined the term. Clams Casino's beats for A$AP Rocky were built from ethereal, reverbed samples of Imogen Heap. Swedish Sad Boys (Yung Lean) and Drain Gang (Bladee, Ecco2k) brought detuned trance synths and emotional vulnerability. The music evokes weightlessness — vast reverb, sparse percussion floating in space. Named for its cloud-like, atmospheric quality.

```
CH: |..x...x...x...x.|
OH: |................|
CP: |....x.......x...|
KK: |X..........x....|
SB: |X..........x....|
PR: |..............x.|
```

Notes: EXTREME REVERB on everything — 4-6 second decay, wet mix at 60-70%. All drum hits should sound distant, as if heard from across a vast empty space. Velocity on all elements is LOW (60-70% max). Hi-hats play only on offbeats, at quarter-note intervals — maximum space between hits. Clap is soft, with long reverb tail that fills the gap before the next hit. Add delay on clap (1/4 note delay, 40% feedback) creating rhythmic echoes. Kick and sub-bass barely present — more felt than heard. The pattern is defined by ABSENCE — what is NOT played matters more than what is. Let the reverb tails fill the space.

### === 19. Type Beat Template (The Generic "Industry" Pattern) ===
Genre: Type Beats
Origin: YouTube/BeatStars, ~2012
BPM: 140
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: "Drake type beat," "Metro Boomin type beat" — an entire production ecosystem where YouTube producers create instrumentals optimized for search algorithms. What began as a sales strategy became a self-reinforcing genre system. This is the STANDARD template that thousands of beats follow — the most common type beat drum pattern. It is competent, functional, and deliberately inoffensive. The musical equivalent of a template resume.

```
CH: |x.xXx.x.x.xXx.x.|
OH: |..............o.|
CP: |....X.......X...|
KK: |X..x......x.X...|
SB: |X..x......x.X...|
```

Notes: This is the STANDARD TRAP TEMPLATE. Hi-hats play 16ths with velocity accents on the "e" of each beat (the 2nd 16th note). Open hi-hat at the end of the bar. Clap on 2 and 4. Kick on beat 1 with a syncopated hit on the "and" of 2 and "e" of 3. 808 bass follows the kick pattern with a pitch slide on the last note. This pattern is designed to be FUNCTIONAL — it leaves space for vocals, sounds "professional," and fits algorithmic playlist criteria. Apply moderate compression. Keep the mix clean. This is optimized for commercial use, not artistic expression.

### === 20. Mashcore / Lolicore ===
Genre: Mashcore / Lolicore
Origin: Japanese internet / BMS communities, late 2000s
BPM: 220
Time Sig: 4/4 (nominally)
Difficulty: Expert
Steps: 32 (two bars at extreme speed)
Cultural Context: Japanese internet breakcore meets anime vocal sampling. Goreshit, Sewerslvt, DJ Sharpnel. The Amen break (from The Winstons' "Amen, Brother") is chopped beyond recognition and reassembled at 200+ BPM. Gabber kicks provide relentless four-on-the-floor drive. At these speeds, individual drum hits blur into continuous textural wash — the listener perceives rhythmic DENSITY rather than discrete beats. Cute anime vocal samples layered over extreme percussion creates intentional tonal whiplash.

```
BAR 1 (Amen break chopped):
CH: |x..xx.x..xx.x..x|
OH: |.......o........|
SN: |.x.X..x.Xx..xX..|
KK: |X..x.X..x.X..x.X|

BAR 2 (Gabber section):
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
OH: |................|
SN: |..x...x...x...x.|
KK: |X.X.X.X.X.X.X.X.|
CR: |X...............|
```

Notes: At 220 BPM, this is EXTREMELY fast. Bar 1 represents a chopped Amen break — the classic breakbeat rearranged into a new pattern. Snare hits are rapid and irregular, following the chopped-break logic. Bar 2 shifts to a gabber section — kick on every 8th note (which at 220 BPM is already overwhelmingly fast), simpler snare pattern. DISTORT the kick heavily — it should function as both bass and kick simultaneously (the "gabber kick" is a pitched, distorted bass drum). Add anime vocal samples as rhythmic punctuation between sections. At these speeds, production clarity is less important than sheer overwhelming intensity.

### === 21. J-Core / Rhythm Game (Camellia Style) ===
Genre: J-Core / Rhythm Game

### === 22. Spotify-Core / Playlist Pop ===
Genre: Playlist Pop
Origin: Spotify algorithmic curation, ~2018
BPM: 108
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: Music shaped by streaming platform algorithms. The 30-second rule — Spotify counts a "stream" at 30 seconds, so hooks must arrive within 5-15 seconds. Skip rate is a key algorithm metric. Average song length on Billboard Top 100 decreased by 20 seconds in 5 years. Production is front-loaded, moderate-tempo, and algorithmically inoffensive. ```

Notes: The pattern is designed for MAXIMUM INOFFENSIVENESS. Moderate tempo (108 BPM). Clean, precisely quantized drums with no intentional humanization. Everything should blend seamlessly into a playlist context — no harsh frequencies, no surprises, no personality. Apply gentle compression. Keep the mix level consistent. ### === 23. ASMR-Pop / Whisper-Pop ===
Genre: ASMR-Pop
Origin: Internet ASMR culture → pop production, ~2018
BPM: 95
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Intermediate
Steps: 16
Cultural Context: Billie Eilish and Finneas brought ASMR (Autonomous Sensory Meridian Response) aesthetics into pop production. On "Bad Guy," finger snaps replace hi-hats. On "Bury a Friend," drums sound like they're being slapped on a tabletop. Some tracks omit percussion entirely. The aesthetic comes from ASMR YouTube — whispering, soft textures, intimate mic proximity. Every sound occurs very close to the listener's ear. This is the opposite of loud.

```
CH: |................|
OH: |................|
SN: |....x...........|
KK: |x...............|
PR: |..x...x.x...x..|
SH: |................|
SB: |x.......x.......|
```

Notes: EXTREMELY quiet and sparse. NO conventional hi-hats. The PR line represents body percussion — finger snaps, mouth clicks, breath sounds, tongue clicks. Velocity on ALL elements is very low (30-50% max). Snare appears only ONCE in the bar. Kick is barely audible — more of a sub-bass pulse (use SB for sub-bass presence). Maximum negative space — more silence than sound. Apply DRY recording on all elements — minimal reverb, creating the sense of sounds happening very close to the listener's ear. The pattern should feel intimate, like someone making quiet sounds in a very small room. Process through gentle saturation for warmth but keep overall level LOW.

### === 24. TikTok Loop ===
Genre: TikTok-Optimized
Origin: TikTok, ~2019
BPM: 112
Time Sig: 4/4
Difficulty: Beginner
Steps: 32 (8 bars — the loop must work as a complete unit)
Cultural Context: TikTok's 15-second format created pressure for music that delivers maximum impact in minimum time. This pattern represents the ideal TikTok sound: immediately catchy, works as a 15-second loop, has a distinctive rhythmic hook, and is dance-friendly (95-125 BPM for choreography that reads well on camera). The hook must establish within the first 2-4 seconds. The entire 8-bar structure should feel like a complete, infinitely loopable musical unit.

```
BAR 1-2 (Hook — the distinctive element):
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|

KK: |X..x..x.X..x..x.|X..x..x.X..x..x.|
PR: |x...........x...|x...........x...|

BAR 3-4 (Build — adding energy):
CH: |x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|x.xXx.xXx.xXx.xX|
OH: |..............o.|..............o.|
CP: |....X.......X...|....X..X..X.X..X|
KK: |X..x..x.X..x..x.|X..x..x.X.X.x.X.|
PR: |x...........x...|x.x.x.x.x.x.x.x.|
CR: |................|X...............|
```

Notes: The first 2 bars establish the hook — a distinctive kick pattern with the signature percussion hit (PR) that makes the pattern immediately recognizable. This is the part that goes viral. Bars 3-4 build energy — hi-hats add rolls, clap adds extra hits, percussion becomes more active. The crash at the start of bar 4 marks the "drop" moment — this is where TikTok creators synchronize their transitions, reveals, or choreography climax. The 4-bar unit (approximately 8.5 seconds at 112 BPM) should feel complete and infinitely loopable. Program a distinctive, memorable rhythmic hook in the PR line — something listeners can identify within 2 seconds. This pattern MUST work as a loop — the transition from bar 4 back to bar 1 should be seamless.
